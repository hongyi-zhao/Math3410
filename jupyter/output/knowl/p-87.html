Let \(V\) be a vector space. If \(V\) can be spanned by a finite number of vectors, then we call \(V\) a finite-dimensional vector space. If \(V\) is finite-dimensional (and non-trivial), and \(\{\vec{e}_1,\ldots, \vec{e}_n\}\) is a basis of \(V\text{,}\) we say that \(V\) has dimension \(n\text{,}\) and write

\begin{equation*} \dim V = n\text{.} \end{equation*}

If \(V\) cannot be spanned by finitely many vectors, we say that \(V\) is infinite-dimensional.
