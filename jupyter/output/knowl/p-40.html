The span of those same vectors is the set of all possible linear combinations:

\begin{equation*} \spn\{\vec{v}_1,\ldots, \vec{v}_k\} = \{c_1\vec{v}_1+ \cdots + c_k\vec{v}_k \,|\, c_1,\ldots, c_k \in \mathbb{F}\}. \end{equation*}

Therefore, the questions “Is the vector \(\vec{w}\) in \(\spn\{\vec{v}_1,\ldots, \vec{v}_k\}\text{?}\)” is really asking, “Can \(\vec{w}\) be written as a linear combination of \(\vec{v}_1,\ldots, \vec{v}_k\text{?}\)”
