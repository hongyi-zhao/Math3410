At an elementary level, Linear Algebra is the study of vectors (in \(\R^n\)) and matrices. Of course, much of that study revolves around systems of equations. Recall that if \(\vec{x}\) is a vector in \(\R^n\) (viewed as an \(n\times 1\) column matrix), and \(A\) is an \(m\times n\) matrix, then \(\vec{y}=A\vec{x}\) is a vector in \(\R^m\text{.}\) Thus, multiplication by \(A\) produces a function from \(\R^n\) to \(\R^m\text{.}\)
